Pricing

Choose the lane that matches the real search workload, not the vague promise of “more”.

PropertyQuarry plans scale on four concrete axes: how many platforms can be searched in one run, how dense the shortlist can become, how high the score ceiling goes, and how deep the research packet should run.

Free proves fit quality first Free keeps broad provider reach but narrows the shortlist aggressively so the first proof is about signal quality, not browsing volume.
Plus buys denser review, not maximum breadth Plus is the focused paid lane: more results per source, a higher threshold ceiling, and richer review packets on a tighter provider set.
Agent unlocks the full property desk Agent restores full-breadth search while increasing shortlist volume and keeping deep research available for the heaviest workflows.

Platform count is not the whole story Provider breadth, shortlist density, and score ceiling are separate levers. The right plan depends on which one is your current bottleneck.
Research depth stays explicit The product should be clear about when a packet is deep, standard, or only a preview, instead of implying every plan gets the same research treatment.
Review page matters The value is not just the crawl. It is the hosted packet, the visible reasoning, and the feedback loop that sharpens the next search run.
How to pick the first paid lane
  • Stay on Free when the main question is whether the ranking model is useful at all.
  • Move to Plus when the first shortlist is too narrow and you need more candidates per source with stronger review pages.
  • Move to Agent when the search needs full source breadth, denser shortlists, and deep research at the same time.
  • Upgrade because of a real workflow bottleneck, not because a generic feature grid says “more”.
What the plans really change
  • How many platforms can be scanned in one run
  • How many candidate listings per source can survive the sweep
  • How high the allowed shortlist threshold can go
  • How deep the packet and follow-up posture should be
  • How much work the review surface can carry before the search becomes noisy again
Good first proof
  • One active workspace
  • One clear property brief
  • One shortlist worth reading end to end
  • One review packet that shows real fit logic
  • One feedback cycle that changes the next search run
What premium should buy
  • More decision-grade candidates, not just more noise
  • Stronger fit thresholds and packet quality
  • Broader crawl coverage only when the search truly needs it
  • Clearer follow-up posture when research gaps are still material
