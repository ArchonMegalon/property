# Seasonal honors and reputation model

## Purpose

This file defines the practical stance for recognition inside BLACK LEDGER and Community Hub.

It complements:

- `NETWORK_REPUTATION_AND_LEADERBOARDS_MODEL.md`
- `REPUTATION_EVENT_LEDGER_MODEL.md`
- `SEASONAL_HONORS_REGISTRY.yaml`

## Canonical rule

Recognition should reward contribution, reliability, and world impact.
It should not become a permanent public shame ladder.

The preferred language is:

- seasonal honors
- reputation
- cred
- notoriety
- runner legend
- faction momentum

## Practical behavior to reward

- GMs who recruit, schedule, and close out cleanly
- players who show up prepared and support the table
- runners whose stories create public-safe memory
- intel contributors whose reports are reviewed and adopted
- faction managers whose moves generate good jobs
- creators whose packets get played and improved

## Hard boundaries

- no permanent all-user ladder at launch
- no public shame boards
- no hidden punitive automation
- no public scoring from Table Pulse or observer tooling
- no spoiler-rich public boards
- no pay-to-win ranking boosts

## First proof gate

**Seattle Season 001 Honors**

Includes:

1. one GM honor
2. one runner legend
3. one intel contributor recognition event
4. one private personal reputation view
5. one public-safe weekly honors summary

Success criterion:

> Users feel recognized for meaningful participation without feeling exposed, punished, or manipulated by an opaque score.
