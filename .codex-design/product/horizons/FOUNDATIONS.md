# Horizon foundations

This file names the present-tense product seams that need to stay strong if Chummer wants its future horizons to become real product lanes instead of attractive dead ends.

## Rule

A horizon may describe future value, but it must not quietly assume magic foundations.
If a future lane depends on a seam, that seam belongs here and in `HORIZON_REGISTRY.yaml`.

## Foundation set

### 1. Expert-speed substrate

The product needs:

* explicit latency budgets for high-frequency build, inspect, compare, and search loops
* keyboard and command-surface seams that can grow into a true expert workflow
* bulk-edit, undo, and cancel-safe transaction boundaries
* dense-state virtualization so large characters, catalogs, and compare views stay fast

This foundation supports `QUICKSILVER`, `ALICE`, and future workbench-heavy lanes.

### 2. Edition-expression substrate

The product needs:

* ruleset-specific terminology seams that are owned, not string-swapped
* UI composition seams for ruleset-specific prompts, inspectors, flows, and theming
* data and explain surfaces that preserve edition-specific meaning where SR4, SR5, and SR6 diverge

This foundation supports `EDITION STUDIO`, flagship authored heads, and future alternate-ruleset work.

### 3. Guided-mastery substrate

The product needs:

* explain receipts that stay readable to non-debuggers
* legality and recommendation seams that stay grounded in rules truth
* starter builds, examples, and recovery flows that can become coachable product surfaces
* bounded recommendation state instead of implicit UI folklore

This foundation supports `ONRAMP`, `ALICE`, and future teaching or primer lanes.

### 4. GM-operations substrate

The product needs:

* campaign, runner, crew, and session state that survives across devices and time
* role-aware permissions and device-role posture
* recap, dossier, and roster surfaces with receipts and publication seams
* a calendar, timeline, or run-control model strong enough to support real prep and live operation

This foundation supports `RUN CONTROL`, `JACKPOINT`, `RUNSITE`, `TABLE PULSE LIVE`, and `TABLE PULSE AFTERMATH`.

### 5. Continuity and degraded-state substrate

The product needs:

* offline-capable local state and reconnect-safe sync bundles
* append-only receipts and replay-safe session/event truth
* visible stale, pending, offline, and conflicted state
* trustworthy crash, update, restore, and handoff recovery posture

This foundation supports `NEXUS-PAN`, `GHOSTWIRE`, and future premium-failure behavior.

### 6. Publication and prestige substrate

The product needs:

* preview-first publication flows
* manifest, provenance, and compatibility attachment for every publishable artifact
* a polished render and theming pipeline for shareable, premium-looking outputs

This foundation supports `JACKPOINT`, `RUNBOOK PRESS`, and future flagship delight lanes.

### 7. Evaluation substrate

The product needs:

* import oracles and parity fixtures
* release gates tied to user-facing outcomes rather than repo-local green status alone
* machine-readable acceptance surfaces that future lanes can cite without reinterpretation

This foundation supports the entire horizon set because it keeps future ambition grounded in falsifiable product evidence.
