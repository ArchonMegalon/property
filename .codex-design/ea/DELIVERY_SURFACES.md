# Delivery Surfaces

Executive Assistant must feel alive outside the browser.
These delivery states are product surfaces, not implementation details.

## Morning memo

The morning memo must have:

- a clear subject line
- one short headline about the day
- a bounded list of what changed
- explicit review items waiting now
- explicit commitments at risk
- one safe path back into the workspace

The memo should answer:
"What changed?", "What needs me?", and "What slips if I ignore this?"

## Approval link

An approval link must show:

- what draft or decision is waiting
- what happens if the user approves
- what happens if the user rejects or defers
- who or what the action affects
- the evidence or source trail behind it

## Expired or invalid link

An expired link must not feel broken.
It must explain:

- why the link no longer works
- whether the item was already resolved
- how to reopen the current workspace safely

## Delivery receipt

A delivery receipt must make transport legible:

- sent
- blocked
- waiting on review
- waiting on reconnect
- failed and retried

Receipts should be short enough for an operator to scan quickly.

## Resend and quiet-hours behavior

Resend behavior must be explicit:

- when a memo is skipped
- when a memo is retried
- when a reminder waits for quiet hours to end
- when a human needs to intervene

Quiet hours are part of trust posture, not only a settings toggle.
